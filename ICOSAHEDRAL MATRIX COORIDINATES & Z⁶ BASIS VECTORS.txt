AXIS 1.4: ICOSAHEDRAL MATRIX COORIDINATES & Z⁶ BASIS VECTORS

1. THE 6D TO 3D PROJECTION VECTORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To project a 6D hyper-cubic integer lattice (Z⁶) into a 3D physical space (E_||) 
while maintaining perfect, long-range icosahedral symmetry, we map the six 
basis vectors of Z⁶ onto six vectors pointing to the vertices of a regular 
icosahedron in 3D space.

Let the 6 basis vectors in physical space be defined as e_n^|| (for n = 1, ..., 6). 
Using the standard Cartesian coordinate axis system, these vectors are defined 
precisely by the transcendental scaling constant φ:

  e_1^|| = N * [  1,   0,  φ ]ᵀ
  e_2^|| = N * [  1,   0, -φ ]ᵀ
  e_3^|| = N * [  0,   φ,  1 ]ᵀ
  e_4^|| = N * [  0,  -φ,  1 ]ᵀ
  e_5^|| = N * [  φ,  1,   0 ]ᵀ
  e_6^|| = N * [ -φ,  1,   0 ]ᵀ

Where N is a normalization constant, defined as N = 1 / √(1 + φ²) = 1 / √(φ + 2), 
ensuring that all projection vectors are unit lengths.

2. THE INNER PRODUCT PROPERTIES (THE GEOMETRIC MATRIX)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Because these six vectors point to the vertices of a regular icosahedron, their 
mutual spatial relationships are heavily restricted by the symmetry operations 
of the point group. The inner product (dot product) between any two distinct 
basis vectors e_i^|| and e_j^|| can only yield three possible values:

  e_i^|| · e_j^|| =  1      (if i = j, identical vertex)
  e_i^|| · e_j^|| =  1/√5   (if the vertices are adjacent)
  e_i^|| · e_j^|| = -1      (if the vertices are directly opposite)

This rigid inner product matrix proves that the structural arrangement is 
completely bound by φ-proportional spacing.
