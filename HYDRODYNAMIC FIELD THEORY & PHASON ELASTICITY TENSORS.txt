AXIS 1.2: HYDRODYNAMIC FIELD THEORY & PHASON ELASTICITY TENSORS
================================================================================

1. THE EXPANDED HYDRODYNAMIC DEGREES OF FREEDOM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When a quasicrystal transitions from an idealized spatial projection to a 
dynamic, load-bearing physical continuum, its thermodynamic description must 
expand. In addition to the standard 3 spatial translation dimensions (phonons), 
the material possesses internal hydrodynamic degrees of freedom (phasons) 
arising from the higher-dimensional parent lattice space.

* Phonon Displacement Field (u_i): Represents the continuous, elastic 
  displacement of atoms from their equilibrium positions. Governed by momentum 
  conservation and propagates as acoustic waves.
  
* Phason Displacement Field (w_i): Represents the local, discontinuous 
  rearrangement of atomic configurations (phason flips). Governed by diffusion 
  mechanics across localized potential barriers.

2. THE COUPLED CONSTITUTIVE EQUATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Under an external stress field, the free energy density (F) of an icosahedral 
quasicrystal cannot be evaluated using standard isotropic Hooke's Law. It is 
expressed as a coupled quadratic expansion of both the symmetric phonon strain 
tensor (ε_ij) and the asymmetric phason strain tensor (ζ_ij):

  F = 1/2 * C_ijkl * ε_ij * ε_kl  +  R_ijkl * ε_ij * ζ_kl  +  1/2 * K_ijkl * ζ_ij * ζ_kl

Where:
  - C_ijkl: Phonon elasticity tensor (governing standard bulk/shear modulus).
  - K_ijkl: Phason elasticity tensor (governing the energy cost of structural shifts).
  - R_ijkl: Phonon-phason coupling tensor (the direct mechanical link where 
            physical loading forces the hyper-dimensional slicing window to tilt).

In a macroscopic icosahedral phase, this field theory reduces the complex 
geometry down to exactly 5 independent elastic constants: 2 for phonons (Lamé 
coefficients), 2 for phasons, and 1 for the direct phonon-phason coupling.

3. PROPAGATION VS. DIFFUSION DYNAMICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Because the physical mechanisms driving u_i and w_i are distinct, their equations 
of motion break down into a coupled hyperbolic-parabolic system:

  Phonon Dynamics (Hyperbolic):  ρ * ü_i = ∂_j ( σ_ij )
  Phason Dynamics (Parabolic):   A * ŵ_i = ∂_j ( H_ij )

Where:
  - ρ is the material density.
  - A is a phason friction coefficient related to the atomic activation barrier.
  - σ_ij is the standard symmetric Cauchy stress tensor.
  - H_ij is the generalized asymmetric phason stress tensor.

Under extreme directional loading, if H_ij exceeds the local activation energy 
threshold, the phason field enters a state of plastic drift. The slicing window 
warps, forcing a cascade of phason flips that alters the local structural density, 
pinning dislocations and fundamentally dictating the material's yield strength.
