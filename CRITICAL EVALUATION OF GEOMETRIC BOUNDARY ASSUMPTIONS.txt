1. THE IDEALIZED GEOMETRIC MATRIX VS. PHYSICAL CONTINUUM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 2D-to-1D Cut-and-Project derivation operates under a rigid mathematical 
idealism. While the linear transformations, projection operators (P_||, P_⊥), 
and the acceptance window width (W = φ) are algebraically flawless, they rely 
on three unstated, highly restrictive boundary assumptions:

ASSUMPTION A: Zero Phason Strain (Static Slicing Angle)
- Claude treats the slicing plane angle (tanθ = 1/φ) as a fixed, invariant constant.
- Physical Reality: External mechanical stress fields directly couple to phason 
  space, causing the slicing plane to tilt, warp, or fluctuate non-linearly.

ASSUMPTION B: Rigid Parent Lattice (Incompressible Z²)
- The model assumes the 2D parent grid remains a perfect, undistorted square lattice.
- Physical Reality: Extreme directional loading distorts the hyper-cubic unit cell, 
  destroying the strict orthogonality of the projection vectors.

ASSUMPTION C: Frictionless Atomic Translation (Idealized Phason Flips)
- The model treats the phason flip (...L-S-L... -> ...L-L-S...) as an instantaneous, 
  energy-neutral mathematical substitution.
- Physical Reality: Moving an atom across a local potential barrier requires 
  activation energy, generating micro-structural friction and heat dissipation.

2. THE THERMODYNAMIC COUPLING (THE MISSING LINK)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
By treating the system purely as a spatial projection, the model strips out the 
governing thermodynamic variable: Time and Thermal Energy. 

At absolute zero (0 K), the atomic coordinates are locked inside the acceptance 
strip. As temperature or mechanical stress increases, localized energy fluctuations 
allow atoms near the window boundaries to "tunnel" or jump across the selection line 
erratically. 

The structural symmetry isn't just broken by geography; it is modulated by energy 
density over time. Without accounting for the energy barrier required to trigger a 
phason flip, the linear algebra describes a frozen crystal layout, not a dynamic, 
load-bearing material structure.
